Sequential Circuits¶

Sequential circuits are digital circuits whose outputs depend not only on their current inputs, but also on the history of inputs - in other words, on their previous states. This gives them a form of “memory.”

Questions¶

1. How exactly is information stored and retained in a latch? Information is stored in an initially inactive (“dead” or “cold”) latch when:

  1. A suitable combination of signals is applied to the input terminals of the latch.

  2. The latch’s internal structure, composed of logic gates (typically NAND or NOR gates), processes these inputs.

  3. The circuit responds by producing outputs at its terminals that reflect the value intended to be stored.

Information is retained in a latch through the following mechanism:

  1. The latch utilizes a cross-coupled structure in its design.

  2. This cross-coupled structure allows the output terminals to continuously reinforce the required input values.

  3. The continuous reinforcement maintains the current output value, effectively “remembering” the stored information.

  4. This feedback loop enables the latch to retain its state even when the original input signals are removed.

  • How does a flip flop store and retain information?

  • How does a flip flop store information? It retains the value between two sampling intervals

  • How does a latch store information? Similar to a flip flop a latch retains its value until a high enable signal makes the latch transparent again to store the current value supplied at its input terminal
